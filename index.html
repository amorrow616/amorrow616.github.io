Who Am I?

A lifelong gamer who is enchanted
by the magic of software engineering.

Hi there! I'm from a small town in upstate New York. Growing up, I had a knack for excelling academically, culminating in my gradutating cum laude from UCF in 2016. As a child, I stumbled upon one of my core passions -- video games. It was through gaming that I discovered excellence is my standard in life, and I will practice relentlessly to achieve it. Guitar Hero? Expert. Hearthstone? Legend. Overwatch? Top 500.

As a software engineer, my superpower is patience. I am patient with stubborn bugs, patient with dissenting opinions on how the code should be structured, and patient when learning new languages and methods. I started 2023 with zero coding experience, and in less than a year have learned enough to build two websites from scratch with fully functional databases, each in a different language. As you can imagine, MDN has become my best friend.

My next conquest? Learning C++. I've been blown away by the incredible things I can do with JavaScript and cannot imagine the thrill of coding a video game. Additionally, I am dedicated to advocating for accessibility, ensuring that the digital landscapes I create are inclusive for everyone.

If I had to choose one word that defines me, it would be "tenacious." When I had to pay my way through college, I balanced working two or three jobs with full-time classes and ended many semesters with a perfect 4.0. At every job, I have been recognized as one of the best employees. When I made the decision that I wanted to be a software engineer, I threw myself into an extremely fast-paced boot camp because I knew that I had the determination and the discipline to make it through.

Am I a hero? ...I really can't say, but yes.

Waterbnb

Inspired by Airbnb, created with JavaScript and React / Redux. Some hightlights:

Leveraged ternary logic to ensure certain buttons are only visible to authorized users, protecting site security.

Enhanced data security by using CSRF tokens to authenticate the user and enforce strict data ownership via 401 errors, ensuring that users cannot modify information that is not authorized to them.
